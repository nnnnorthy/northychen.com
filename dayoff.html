DAY-OFF
2009

These days we are surrounded by so many electronic products that need to be charged or plugged in. However, people keep over-charging their electronics and wasting electricity.

Day-Off is a product that I worked on during my sophomore year.
It is a product that has seven electrical sockets which correspond to the seven days of the week. These sockets take turns switching off for a day. For example, on each Thursday, the fifth socket will take a "day-off" while the other six are fully functional. It's a small gesture to remind people that saving energy is essential in order to give our planet a break.

Additionally, the Day-Off has an LCD screen on the side
that shows energy consumption for the day, and also displays
the date and time.
