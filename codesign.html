CO-DESIGN BOX
2010

In collaboration with Felipe Sarmiento.

It was a series of design workshops with four groups of middle school students (14 total) in Providence, Rhode Island as clients and test subjects. Their tasks were to figure out a daily problem at school and then solve it by redesigning it.

What resulted from the co-design workshops was a set of collaborative design tools that improved our brainstorming sessions, simplified the design process and helped non-designers understand what it took to design an object.

At the end, four groups of the co-design students achieved to propose re-design ideas: tackle the school cafeteria waiting line issue, locker room with more privacy, toilet seat with thermal sensor, and more stylish school bag design.
