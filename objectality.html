OBJECTALITY
2012

As the initial research chapter of my MFA thesis at RISD, I coined the word "Objectality", to describe an object's personality. I illustrated short narratives for all of my personal belongings and divided them into different categories based on my interaction with them. I thought that the interaction between humans and objects could be like a consistent loop - full of imaginary stories. I chose several objects I felt most attached to, and developed short video prototype for each.

For instance, electronic cords, which I find are objects that desire human beings' caress. A person put the charger, the mouse, all the usb wires individually into a backpack, with the intention of separating them. However, somehow they twist and hook with each other in the darkness. At the end, the person need to spend time with the cords untying all of them. It's almost like a private joke the cords are playing with the person, which is like a way of flirting.

Or, a sofa that records the presence of a person. It recalls the shapes and indentations left from that person and plays this memory back with a higher frequency in that person’s absence. It reminds people of the missed presence of the person who is gone.
