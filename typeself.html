TYPESELF
2015

Each Wieden+Kennedy office around the world has a unique way to display the employees’ profiles. Usually the profile picture wall is a good demonstration about each office’s culture and value. The Shanghai office’s is out of date, and we decided to relaunch a self-updating system to deal with the problem.

We found some old typewriters and bought them on Taobao. At the end, one of those old machines was born with a new life and became a portrait generator. It kept typing people's faces really loud for the whole summer.

We tore up five typewriters to study the typewriter's basic mechanics. We also explored their different system fonts by scanning and dividing the characters to finally pick one as our original font model. We then programmed these individual elements to allow the machine to automatically type the characters, using them as brushstrokes to create the unique combinations that form the portraits.

We printed invitation cards with the new portrait collection of W+K Shanghai, and held a small gallery show on Changle Road in Shanghai. The Creators Project of VICE gave us a big shout.
