TYPESELF
2015

Each Wieden+Kennedy office around the world has a unique way to display the employees’ profiles. Usually the profile picture wall is a good demonstration about each office’s culture and value.
The Shanghai office’s is out of date, and we decided to relaunch
a self-updating system to deal with the problem and express
the office’s voice out and loud.

We use an analog approach to visually present the image of Wieden+Kennedy Shanghai in the crazily digitalized environment of China. We choose a hacked typewriter to output all the employees’ portraits to imply the provocative creativeness of Wieden+Kennedy’s culture stands. The Typeself machine and the program will then self-generate a new typeself profile for each new employee who joins the WK Shanghai Office.

The making of Typeself involved tearing up, decoding, and hacking five typewriters to lead the words to deconstruction. We explored their different system fonts by scanning and dividing the characters to finally pick one as our original font model. We then programmed these individual elements to allow the machine to automatically type the characters, using them as the brushstrokes to create the unique combinations that form the portraits.

Based on the implication and systematic usage of characters, we used strategic wordplay for the Chinese translation of Typeself. “打字机” is the Chinese word for “typewriter”. However, using similar pronunciation but different characters, “打自己” actually works to define our new visual system, literally translating to “hit myself” - exactly how we used our machine: by hitting the letters to create portraits of one’s self.
